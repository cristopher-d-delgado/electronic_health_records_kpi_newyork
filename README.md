# Hospital Discharge Analysis: Trends, Costs, and Outcomes in New York State

**Author:** Cristopher Delgado

## Data Source

Statewide Planning and Research Cooperative System (SPARCS) Inpatient De-identified File (healthdata.gov); [Hospital Inpatient Discharges 2024](https://healthdata.gov/State/Hospital-Inpatient-Discharges-SPARCS-De-Identified/pqtp-sb82/about_data).

## Purpose

Analyze hospital discharge data across New York to understand trends in healthcare utilization, cost drivers, patient demographics, and outcomes. Translate these insights into actionable metrics for hospital administrators and public health decision makers.

## Buisness Questions

How do hospital performance, cost structures, patient demographics, and outcomes interact across New York State hospitals, and what insights can be derived to improve healthcare efficiency and patient care?

### Hospital Performance & Utilization

How efficiently are hospitals across New York utilizing resources, and which facilities or regions show patterns of higher discharge volumes, longer stays, or higher readmission rates?

**Key Performance Indicators (KPI):**

* Total Discharges per Hospital
* Average Length of Stay
* Discharges per Diagnosis Group

### Cost & Charges Analysis

What are the major drivers of hospital charges across New York, and how do costs vary by diagnosis, payer type, and hospital?

**Key Performance Indicators (KPI):**

* Average Total Charges (by Diagnosis, Payer, and Hospital)
* Top 10 Costliest Diagnoses
* Average Cost per Day
* Correlation between Average Length of Stay and Total Charges

### Patient Demographics & Health Trends

What are the key demographic patterns among hospitalized patients in New York, and how do age, gender, and region influence diagnosis trends and hospital utilization?

**Key Performance Indicators (KPI):**

* Discharges by Age Group and Gender 
* Top Diagnoses by Age Group and Gender 
* Discharges by County or Region

**4. Outcomes & Quality of Care** 

What factors are associated with patient outcomes such as in-hospital mortality and length of stay, and how do these outcomes vary across hospitals and diagnoses?

**Key Performance Indicators (KPI):**

* Average LOS by Outcome
